POSITUDE

For university students who are seeking mental health support, Positude is a mobile application that measures user emotion and offers personalized mental coping strategies powered by AI.

Empathy

1. Background

Mental health issues have become a significant and growing concern for university students. According to American College Health Association (2019) on health assessment for Canadian universities, 51.6% of students reported that they had experienced depression within the last year, 68.9% had felt overwhelming anxiety, and 87.6% had been mentally exhausted. Although many college students are struggling with different mental health issues, the assessment has shown that the majority of them (67%) never asked for professional support.

University students can be detrimentally impacted by mental health issues on their academic performance and life satisfaction (Arslan et al., 2009). The most common mental health problems in students include depression, eating disorders, and sleep difficulty, which may prevent them from reaching short-term and long-term educational goals. Besides, university students suffering from depression are more likely to overindulge in drinking and smoking, lose concentration, and experience difficulties in emotional control. On top of that, suicide can be one of the most serious consequences of long-term depression among university students (Yusoff et al., 2013).

2. Pains

Unawareness

Many students are not aware of the on-campus counseling services (Beebe, 2017). Eisenberg et al. (2007) found that 51% of students did not know where to go for mental care, and 41% were unaware of free counseling services offered on campus. Moreover, many students do not even understand and recognize their mental health problems. Students often perceive high-level stress and anxiety as normal for university life, so they do not think mental health support is necessary (Sasaki, 2014). Even though mental supporting services have proven to be an effective use (Sharkin, 2004; Hinderaker, 2013), the programs are often underutilized by university students (D’Amico et al., 2016).

Stigma

The other notable barrier to access mental health support is self-stigma and perceived stigma (Gulliver et al., 2010; Lee & Jung, 2018). Self-stigma happens when individuals have negative attitudes towards themselves (Sussman et al., 1987); for example, they will regard depression as a result of their weak personalities. Perceived stigma refers to perceptions of negative responses from others (Barney et al., 2006); people might suppose the counselor looks down on them while asking for help. These two types of stigma explain why university students are reluctant to approach face-to-face counseling services for help.

3. User Group

The project is intended to identify the obstacles that limit university students from seeking mental services, and thus to provide them with a rich amount of information to consult with. However, our information service program is not designed to intervene in a mental health crisis. Based on the research, our team concluded a persona to establish empathy with their frustrations and identify their needs.

There wasn’t enough time to process ethics reviews within the course project, therefore, surveys were not conducted to collect data from targeted users. Through intensive literature review, our team has determined the user group of this project to be university students who need mental health support.

Prototype

Privacy Concerns

Positude limits the information required for signing up and thus does not require personally identifiable information like legal name and phone number to create an account.

For the ‘Nearyby’ page, we abandoned the use of open-sourced API and finalized our design into a virtual radar view. The users’ exact location will not be shown on the app or even collected by a third party.

Accessibility

To improve the accessibility and readability of the interface design, our team used font sizes larger than 12. We also included assistant technology like voice input to help those with difficulty in typing. In addition, an SOS button was added to the home screen in case users may need to call the crisis line in a timely manner.

Marketing

We will use social media marketing like Instagram Ads to promote brand awareness and on-campus workshops to drive conversion. The in-app reward system helps to increase user retention by encouraging them to check in on a consecutive basis. The invite friends feature on the setting page as an incentivized sharing will assist our referral marketing campaign. Finally, the Positude Plus subscription contributes to our revenue model and converts the users into loyal customers.
